The Global and United Kingdom Burden of Tuberculosis

Tuberculosis (TB) is a historic infectious disease that remains an urgent public health priority today [1]. While the diagnosis may feel isolating, TB is entirely preventable and curable with the right medical care [2]. By understanding the true scale of this illness—globally, nationally, and locally—you can navigate your recovery knowing you are supported by advanced, dedicated healthcare resources [3].

  • Annual Impact: In the past year, an estimated 10.8 million people fell ill with the disease globally [3].
  • Concentrated Risk: 87% of all cases are concentrated in just 30 high-burden nations, primarily across Asia and Africa [4].
  • Medical Success: Thanks to prompt medical treatment and improved rapid diagnostic testing, global medical efforts have successfully saved roughly 83 million lives since the year 2000 [4]. The global treatment success rate for standard forms of the disease remains highly effective at 88% [4].
  • National Incidence: England currently sits at 9.4 cases per 100,000 people, classifying it as a low-incidence country by global standards [5].
  • Recent Increases: In 2024, there were 5,490 notified cases in England, marking a 13.6% jump from the previous year—the largest annual rise since national tracking began in 1971 [5].
  • Social Factors: The condition is deeply linked to social and health inequalities, heavily impacting marginalized groups and urban areas. Data indicates 81.9% of affected individuals in England were born outside the UK [5].
  • Local Rates: Leicester currently reports the highest rate of the disease in all of England, with a three-year average of 42.1 cases for every 100,000 people [3].
  • Diagnostic Delays: Locally, one in three patients experiences a delayed diagnosis of over four months, which can lead to severe illness and high rates of emergency hospital admissions [3].
  • Dedicated Support: To combat this and support patients effectively, local NHS centers have developed “Rapid Access Pathways” [3]. Clinics now employ diverse nursing teams and cultural link workers to ensure you receive urgent, understandable, and culturally sensitive care throughout your treatment [3].

Key Takeaways

  • TB is a completely curable bacterial infection; current global medical treatments have an 88% success rate [4].
  • The UK has seen a recent rise in cases, largely clustered in urban areas and driven by health and social inequalities [5].
  • While Leicester has the highest notification rate in England, local healthcare systems have implemented specialized clinical pathways and dedicated teams to ensure you receive rapid, effective care [3].
